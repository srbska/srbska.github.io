SRBSKA.ORG

How can one remember a place which exists in many memories, in many stories?

The village of Srbská is a tiny place in northern Czech Republic, border to Poland. Today it has just a handful of houses but prior to 1946 it was a thriving village with hundreds of residents, a village which used to be named Wünschendorf, which translates from German as ‘wish village’.

When I moved there into the village in 2007 I had yet to uncover the rich tapestry of stories holding the past of this now seemingly empty location. This archive is an attempt to ensure this past can be preserved and experienced by a wider audience.

Contents

Over the past decade I’ve travelled to northern Germany and the Czech Republic to film interviews with people who were exiled from the village of Wünschendorf in 1946 and also the residents who have lived in the village up until the present day.

Through this process I’ve also accumulated hundreds of photographs and archival documents, all of which will be displayed in srbska.org.
